Kaiyuan Tan is a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at Vanderbilt University, advised by Professor Thomas Beckers. Prior to joining Vanderbilt, he completed his M.S. in Electrical Engineering at Washington University in St. Louis with thesis in 2023, where he worked with Professor Yiannis Kantaros. He earned his B.Eng. in Information Engineering from Sun Yat-Sen University, China, in 2021. Where he was selected as the member of extraordinary class.

Kaiyuan's research interests span robotics, decision under uncertainty quantification, and machine learning. His current research focused on advancing physically accurate, learning-based modeling and safe control of physical and robotic systems. His research goal is to advance of safe and meaningful interactions between robot and human, ensuring that robot systems can maintain stability and avoid failures in the context of uncertainty. He has published in top venues such as ICRA, L4DC, and IFAC.
