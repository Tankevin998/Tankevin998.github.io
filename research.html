Research Highlights

My research goal focus on the safe, data-driven modeling and control of robotic systems. This motivates novel methods that leverage learning-based approaches while ensuring the safe operation and high performance of robotic control systems. These methods are designed to operate in dynamic and uncertain environments, addressing key challenges faced by robots as they interact with the physical world.

Under such context, my current research centers on physics-informed learning, which embeds physical principles into machine learning models to combine the strengths of data-driven approaches with domain-specific knowledge. By integrating these physical laws, the models are not only more robust and interpretable but also retain the flexibility and adaptability of traditional data-driven methods. This synergy enhances the overall performance, providing a reliable framework for modeling complex systems where conventional machine learning may fall short.

Additionally, my work extends to learning-enabled control, where these enhanced models are then used to develop intelligent control strategies. By leveraging physics-informed learning, I aim to create control algorithms that are not only data-efficient but also capable of ensuring stability, safety, and performance, which is crucial for robots to nicely interact with environment and human.

Aligned with the theme of modeling complex systems, I have also worked in mathematical biology where I studied mathematical properties of ODE/PDE models and developed novel models for disease population dynamics. This work shares a common foundation with my research in physics-informed learning, as both rely on the mathematics of dynamical systems to capture complex behaviors.
